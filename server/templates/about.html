About Hyperindex

Hyperindex is a small project built around a simple idea: keep track of websites you like, and share the ones you think are worth revisiting.

The Yellow Pages is a shared collection made up of submissions from different users. Entries go through a quick admin review before they're visible to everyone. This keeps things lightweight and avoids spam or broken pages.

For personal archiving, the site also features a Rolodex, where you save links, jot down notes, and tag things in a way that makes sense to you. None of this is public unless you choose to submit an entry.

There's no feed, no algorithm, and no real pressure to “engage.” Hyperindex doesn't try to replace bookmarks or browsing — it's more like a public notepad for the web. If you remember old directories, zine-style link pages, or early blogs with a sidebar full of links — that's the vibe.

It's intentionally plain. The goal is to keep it usable and human without much overhead.

The project is open-source and designed for longevity, transparency, and fun. Whether you're into digital gardening, link rot prevention, or just miss the old internet, Hyperindex is a place to build your own slice of it.

Questions or feedback? Visit the GitHub repo.
